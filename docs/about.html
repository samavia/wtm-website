About

Mission

Women Techmakers is a global program created by Google to highlight the talent of women in technology. Together with other sponsors and partners Google is supporting the development of women in technology. Women Techmakers Hamburg is independent from Google and our other partners. The events and speakers that we selected are based our ideas and knowledge. This community promotes talented and passionate women and increases the visibility of the Hamburg tech community while honoring women in technology. We want to provide visibility, community and resources for women interested in tech.

Values

Respect, trust, fairness, embrace differences, sharing and supporting, improvement. Our values is what we think everyone should be based on. They are also what we use to benchmark our actions. We want everyone respected as a person, having them trusted, treated in a fair manner. We want to have equality in value while understanding the differences - we think that using different mindsets together delivers way better quality. We want information to be shared and colleagues to be supported, all for the purpose of continuous improvement.

Vision

A career or education choice in STEM has to be independent of gender identity.

Meet the Team

Anja Schumann
WTM Lead Women Techmakers Hamburg

Anja decided to launch Women Techmakers in Hamburg in October last year. Because she wanted to found her own tech startup she participated the tech meetup scene and was shocked about the not existing diversity. She decided to do something, reached out to Google and here we are today. She has a background in business informatics. She was responsible for transforming processes with the help of information systems in companies like Lenovo and Tchibo before she decided to start with her own company Fashion Fellows. She loves to travel and lived some to time in Israel and the United States and knows how inspiring startup eco systems can be like.

Alisa Trunczik
Co-Organizer Women Techmakers Hamburg

Alisa can be a good Role Model for all of you who want to want to have a career in software development. She is an electrical engineer and started to learn web development last summer to become a Front end Developer in the future. She originally comes from Siberia, Russia and just moved to Hamburg in the beginning of 2015. Right now Alisa works as an Antenna Network Support for Vesseltracker.com, where she can use her both technical and communication skills and continues gaining experience in programming. She is obsessed with technologies, new languages, traveling and coffee. Alisa wants to encourage more women to code and create awesome things.
